Kepler


- Kepler is a sustainable operations and energy management platform designed to provide insights and analysis for sustainability initiatives, particularly targeting edge computing and remote infrastructure.
- ZAGA's integration with Kepler enables the extension of sustainability monitoring to distributed edge environments, allowing organizations to track and manage energy consumption and sustainability metrics at the edge, in addition to centralized facilities.
- Kepler is a project developed through a collaboration between Red Hat and IBM Research, aiming to estimate system energy consumption in a Kubernetes environment. The project utilizes CPU performance counters and machine learning models to approximate the power usage of individual pods, workloads, namespaces, or entire Kubernetes clusters. This data is then exported as Prometheus metrics, enabling end users to monitor container energy consumption and make informed decisions to achieve energy conservation goals.
- The adoption of Kepler is particularly important in edge computing, where minimizing environmental impact and reducing operational costs are crucial. Efficient power consumption can lead to significant cost savings, and sustainable practices can help extend the lifespan of edge devices and reduce electronic waste. Kepler's integration in edge computing environments not only promotes energy-efficient practices but also contributes to the broader goal of sustainability in technology.
- Additionally, the project offers an easy installation process and provides the OpenTelemetry Collector (OTC) as a lightweight option for exporting metrics in edge environments. By utilizing Grafana and the Prometheus DataSource in Red Hat OpenShift, users can visualize Kepler metrics, ultimately allowing for thorough monitoring and visualization of energy consumption data.
- For a comprehensive understanding of Kepler, including its architecture, design, and roadmap, users can refer to the project's documentation or participate in bi-weekly community meetings to engage further with this innovative initiative.

OpenTelemetry


- OpenTelemetry is an open-source project for observability that enables the collection of telemetry data, including metrics, traces, and logs, from various sources within an organization's infrastructure.
- ZAGA's integration with OpenTelemetry supports the collection and transmission of sustainability-related telemetry data, allowing for seamless integration with ZAGA's monitoring and reporting systems. This enables holistic sustainability management by capturing and analyzing observability data aligned with sustainable operations.
- OpenTelemetry is a powerful project that has been incubated by the Cloud Native Computing Foundation (CNCF). It is designed to provide a collection of tools, APIs, and SDKs for instrumenting, generating, collecting, and exporting telemetry data such as metrics, logs, and traces. This enables users to effectively analyze the performance and behavior of their software systems.
- In edge computing environments, where there are often limitations on CPU, memory, and disk space, traditional monitoring stacks can be resource-intensive. OpenTelemetry addresses this challenge by offering a lightweight solution for exporting metrics. The OpenTelemetry Collector (OTC), a key component of the project, acts as a vendor-agnostic proxy capable of receiving, processing, and exporting telemetry data in various formats, including Prometheus metrics.
- By utilizing the OTC, users can efficiently export telemetry data, ensuring it is effectively delivered to the desired destinations for analysis and visualization. In combination with Prometheus metrics generated by tools such as Kepler, the OTC provides valuable support for monitoring and managing the energy consumption of edge devices and workloads.
- Ultimately, OpenTelemetry plays a crucial role in enabling comprehensive and efficient observability in edge computing environments, contributing to improved performance, resource management, and sustainability.

Grafana


- Grafana is a leading open-source platform for monitoring and observability, known for its flexible and comprehensive visualization and monitoring capabilities.
- With Grafana, users can create dynamic and customizable dashboards to visually represent various data sources, including sustainability metrics, energy consumption, and environmental telemetry.
- Grafana's integration with ZAGA allows for the creation of visually intuitive interfaces that facilitate the real-time tracking and visualization of sustainability metrics and performance indicators.

Prometheus


- Prometheus is an open-source monitoring and alerting toolkit focused on collecting, storing, and querying time-series data. It is widely used for monitoring cloud-native environments and infrastructure.
- Through integration with Prometheus, ZAGA facilitates the comprehensive scraping, storage, and querying of sustainability-related metrics, enabling the analysis of historical trends and the generation of customizable sustainability reports for informed decision-making.

By leveraging the capabilities of Grafana, Kepler, Prometheus, and OpenTelemetry, ZAGA provides a comprehensive and integrated approach to sustainability monitoring, visualization, telemetry collection, and analysis, empowering organizations to effectively manage and improve their sustainability practices and environmental impact reduction initiatives.
